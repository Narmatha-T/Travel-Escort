Rules of Photography

There are six basic rules of Photography

  • Rule of Thirds
  • Simplify the images
  • Fill the frame
  • Use leading lines
  • Keep horizons straight
  • Use the camera grid

The rule of thirds is a composition guideline that places your subject in the left or right third of an image, leaving the other two thirds more open.

Leading lines are lines that lead from the foreground to the background of the scene. A leading line could be a path, a road, a river, row of trees or a railway line.
